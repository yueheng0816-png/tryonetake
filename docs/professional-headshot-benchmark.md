## The Gold Standard: What Pros Actually Deliver

### Peter Hurley (NYC/LA) — The Industry Benchmark

- **Style**: Crisp, modern, clean white backgrounds, confident expressions
- **Signature technique**: The "squinch" — slightly narrowing lower eyelids for confident, engaged look
- **Posing**: Extended jawline, "forehead towards camera," slight lower shooting angle for authority
- **Lighting**: Soft, even Kino Flo or CineFlex; minimal shadows; dimensional but flattering
- **Crop**: Horizontal orientation, tight crop (top of head often cut), eyes above centerline
- **Background**: Primarily clean white (timeless) or matte black
- **Pricing**: Sessions from $500, retouched images from $150 each
- **Book**: *"The Headshot"* — definitive industry guide
- **Headshot Crew**: Global network of photographers trained in his methodology → consistent look across locations

**Key takeaway**: Hurley's style is the most-copied in corporate America. White background + confident squinch = the "LinkedIn look" everyone expects.

## How Real Sessions Are Structured

Based on research across 10+ professional headshot studios:

### Typical Session Packages

| Session Tier | Duration | Outfits | Backgrounds | Final Images | Price Range |
|-------------|----------|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Basic/Express** | 30 min | 1 | 1 (white) | 1-3 retouched | $150-300 |
| **Standard** | 45-60 min | 2-3 | 2 (white + gray) | 3-5 retouched | $300-500 |
| **Premium/Branding** | 75-90 min | 3-4 | 3+ (studio + environmental) | 15-40 | $500-950 |
| **Executive/Full** | 2-3 hours | 4-5 | Multiple locations | 20-40+ | $950-2,500+ |

### What Pros Recommend Clients Bring

- **3-5 outfits**: Business formal, business casual, smart casual, creative
- **"Jacket-on, jacket-off"** strategy: Two looks from one outfit
- **Accessories**: Tie/no tie, cardigan switch, jewelry change for variety
- **Backdrop variety**: White/light gray for LinkedIn, dark for executive, environmental for personality

## Professional Headshot Style Categories

### 1. The Corporate Classic (50% of all professional headshots)

```
SETTING:      Clean white or light gray seamless background
LIGHTING:     Two-light setup — key light 45°, fill light opposite side
WARDROBE:     Dark suit (navy/charcoal), white or light blue shirt
EXPRESSION:   Confident closed-lip smile, direct eye contact
FRAMING:      Shoulders-up, slight angle (not straight-on)
USE CASE:     LinkedIn, company website, email signature
```

**Reference photographers**: Peter Hurley, Executive Lens, HeadShots Inc

### 2. The Environmental Executive (20%)

```
SETTING:      Office environment — desk, window, bookshelf, lobby
LIGHTING:     Natural window light + subtle fill from office lighting
WARDROBE:     Full suit or blazer, sometimes with coat on shoulder
EXPRESSION:   Authoritative but warm, slight knowing smile
FRAMING:      Waist-up or 3/4 body, subject occupies 60% of frame
USE CASE:     "About Us" pages, speaker bios, press kits
```

### 3. The Modern Tech / Startup (15%)

```
SETTING:      Open office, brick wall, rooftop, coffee shop, lounge
LIGHTING:     Natural light dominant, casual window-side posing
WARDROBE:     Smart casual — blazer over tee, button-down no tie, polo, fine knit
EXPRESSION:   Relaxed genuine smile, engaged, sometimes candid mid-conversation
FRAMING:      Waist-up, sometimes walking or mid-action
USE CASE:     Startup team pages, tech conference bios, social media
```

### 4. The Creative / Editorial (10%)

```
SETTING:      Gallery, colorful backdrop, architectural space, urban night
LIGHTING:     Dramatic — rim light, gel colors, chiaroscuro, neon
WARDROBE:     Fashion-forward, textured fabrics, bold colors, leather jacket
EXPRESSION:   Intense, mysterious, joyful — personality-driven
FRAMING:      Variety — tight face to full-body environmental
USE CASE:     Creative industry, personal brand, speaking engagements
```

### 5. The Outdoor Lifestyle (5%)

```
SETTING:      Park, campus, beach, mountain, city street, harbor
LIGHTING:     Golden hour (1hr before sunset), overcast (soft diffused)
WARDROBE:     Smart casual with climate-appropriate layers
EXPRESSION:   Genuine, relaxed, natural — less "posed"
FRAMING:      Waist-up to full-body, subject in context
USE CASE:     Real estate, coaching, wellness, outdoor industries
```

## Complete Portfolio: What a "Full Session" Looks Like

A premium headshot session typically delivers variety across these dimensions:

### Wardrobe Categories
| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **Formal business** | Dark suit + white/blue shirt + tie | Blazer + silk blouse or sheath dress |
| **Business casual** | Blazer + open collar, no tie | Cardigan + blouse, structured top |
| **Smart casual** | Button-down rolled sleeves, fine knit | Cashmere sweater, elegant top |
| **Creative** | Dark tee + jacket, textured blazer | Statement piece, bold color/pattern |
| **Seasonal layer** | Trench coat, wool coat, scarf | Coat, wrap, seasonal accessories |

### Background Types
| Type | % of Portfolio | Best For |
|------|---------------|----------|
| White seamless | 40% | LinkedIn, corporate sites |
| Light gray seamless | 15% | Versatile, softer than white |
| Dark gray/black | 10% | Executive, dramatic |
| Colored (teal, navy, etc.) | 5% | Brand-aligned, creative |
| Office environmental | 15% | "About Us," team pages |
| Outdoor natural | 10% | Lifestyle, approachability |
| Architectural/urban | 5% | Creative, modern |

### Pose Categories
| Pose | Frequency | Expression Pairing |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Straight-on, shoulders-up | 35% | Warm smile or neutral confident |
| 45° angle, looking at camera | 25% | Engaged, approachable |
| Seated (chair, desk, steps) | 15% | Relaxed, thoughtful |
| Standing full-body | 10% | Authoritative, dynamic |
| Walking (mid-stride) | 5% | Natural, candid |
| Leaning (wall, railing, desk) | 5% | Casual confidence |
| Arms crossed | 3% | Power, authority |
| Over-the-shoulder glance | 2% | Editorial, dramatic |

### Lighting Setups
| Setup | Use Case |
|-------|----------|
| **Clamshell** (key above, fill below) | Beauty, classic headshot |
| **Three-point** (key + fill + rim) | Corporate standard |
| **Butterfly/Paramount** (key directly above) | Glamour, polished |
| **Single key + natural fill** | Environmental, window-lit |
| **Rim/silhouette** | Dramatic, artistic |
| **Golden hour backlight** | Outdoor warmth |
| **Chiaroscuro** (single source, deep shadow) | Fine art, moody |

## Key Insights for OneTake Prompt Design

### What We Get Right
- ✅ 64 templates cover all 5 major style categories
- ✅ Studio classic (12) + outdoor (13) = strong foundation
- ✅ Executive category captures premium settings well
- ✅ Prompt structure (setting + outfit + expression + lighting) mirrors pro sessions

### Critical Missing Elements

**1. High-key white background is underrepresented.** It's the #1 most-requested headshot type in corporate America, yet we only have 3-4 templates with explicit white studio backgrounds.

**2. Gendered outfit specificity is minimal.** Pro sessions offer different guidance for men vs women. Our templates are mostly unisex/generic. We need explicit female-coded (blouse, sheath dress, silk, jewelry) and male-coded (suit+tie, cufflinks, pocket square) variants.

**3. Industry-specific templates are missing:**
- Healthcare (scrubs, white coat, stethoscope)
- Legal (robe, law library, gavel)
- Real estate (property background, warm smile)
- Trades (hard hat, workshop)

**4. "Jacket-on, jacket-off" — we have no same-pose-different-outfit pairs, which pros use heavily for efficiency.**

### Recommendations for v3

| Priority | Action | Templates |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| 🔴 HIGH | Add high-key white bg variants | 4-6 new |
| 🔴 HIGH | Add gendered outfit variants | 6-8 new |
| 🟡 MED | Add healthcare + legal industry | 4 new |
| 🟡 MED | Add arms-crossed variants (popular) | 2-3 new |
| 🟢 LOW | Add "jacket-off" relaxed pairs | 2-3 new |
| 🟢 LOW | Add group/team subtle hint | 1-2 experimental |
